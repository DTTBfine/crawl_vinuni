December 7, 2024

Your Learning Journey Doesn’t Always Have to Follow a Straight Line” – Prof. Kristi S. Anseth

Live at VinUni, we had the privilege of engaging in profound dialogues with the 2024 VinFuture Laureates. In the first session, a compelling discussion unfolded, delving into the beginnings of some of the greatest scientific minds of our time, touching on a relatable and practical topic for university students and young people: CAREER CHOICES AND THE ROLE OF MENTORS.

As world-renowned scientists, what were the uncertain moments like when these professors had to make critical decisions at the start of their careers?  
 What were the key factors or defining moments that made them realize they had chosen the right path?  
 How can one find great mentors throughout life’s journey?

These questions were answered live by leading scientists from around the globe, including:  
 Prof. Kristi S. Anseth (USA) – Recipient of the 2024 VinFuture Special Prize for Female Scientists for her groundbreaking advancements in polymer material design and biomedical applications.  
 Prof. Carl H. June (USA) and Prof. Michel Sadelain (USA) – Recipients of the 2024 VinFuture Special Prize for Pioneers in Emerging Fields for their pioneering work in the discovery and development of CAR-T cell therapy for cancer and other diseases.

## VinUni Community Donates 1,000 EZMeal Vouchers to Support VinUni Students

Dear parents, students, and our beloved community,

Every VinUni student is a remarkable talent with dreams, aspirations, and the determination to strive for excellence. Yet, despite their best efforts, some students face financial challenges that may interrupt their studies or even force them to put their future on hold.

With the hope of standing by these young talents, VinUni Community Board of Directors (BOD) has launched the **Student Cares Fund**, calling for the collective support of our community to ensure that no student is left behind due to hardship.

* **Total amount received:** 45,000,000 VND (from three parents within our BOD)
* **Total amount disbursed:** 5,000,000 VND (supported one student in purchasing a laptop through the Financial Aid Office – FAO)
* **Remaining balance:** 40,000,000 VND

The BOD has decided to donate the remaining 40 million VND to provide 1,000 EZMeal lunch vouchers, distributed via FAO to students with financial need in alignment with the fund’s original purpose.

VinUni Community extends our heartfelt gratitude to the parents who have accompanied the Student Cares Fund throughout its operation. Your generosity not only provides timely support to our students but also reflects the deep compassion of our community, standing together with the VinUni in nurturing Vietnam’s future changemakers.

With warmest appreciation,   
VinUni Community Executive Board

## Higher Education in the Age of AI & VUCA – How Should Parents Prepare Their Children?

March 27, 2025

On the morning of March 24, as part of the preliminary round of the National & International Academic Olympic Competition hosted at VinUni, hundreds of outstanding high school students and parents from across the country gathered on campus. On this occasion, VinUni Community organized a Community Talk titled **“Higher Education in the Age of AI & VUCA”**, aiming to help parents better understand the rapid changes in education and the labor market, and how to best prepare their children for the future.

**The World Is Changing – Parents Need to Rethink How They Prepare Their Children**

With the rapid advancement of AI and technology, alongside an increasingly unpredictable world defined by VUCA (Volatility, Uncertainty, Complexity, Ambiguity), many skills and types of knowledge once considered “keys to success” may no longer be sufficient for the future.

So the question becomes:

**How Can Students Be Equipped to Step Confidently into the Future?**

From this starting point, the speakers who are educational experts, university lecturers, and parents themselves had shared practical, multi-dimensional perspectives:

**Dr. Giáp Văn Dương** – Chairman of VinUni Community, Education Expert   
 **Ms. Trịnh Thị Thúy Liên, MA** – Lecturer, College of Liberal Arts & Sciences, Head of Amser @VinUni Community Chapter   
 **Dr. Lê Duy Dũng** – Program Director, Bachelor of Data Science, College of Engineering & Computer Science

**Key Takeaways from the Talk**:

**1. How Is the World Changing with AI & VUCA?**

* AI is not only changing how people work, but also reshaping the global labor market.

* Traditional jobs are gradually disappearing, while new roles continue to emerge.

* Flexibility and adaptability are becoming essential survival skills.

**2. How Should Students Learn in the Age of AI?**

* A shift from rote learning to thinking, creativity, and problem-solving is crucial.

* AI can enhance learning efficiency, but also raises challenges around critical thinking and ethics.

* Integrating technology into learning is no longer optional; it’s inevitable.

**3. The Role of Higher Education – Choosing the Right University and Major**

* Universities are no longer just places that deliver knowledge; they help students build skills, innovative thinking, and a lifelong learning mindset.

* The value of a university lies not only in its degree, but also in its networks, learning environment, and opportunities for holistic development.

**4. What Can Parents Do to Support Their Children?**

* Guide without imposing – help children explore their interests and potential instead of chasing “trending” majors.

* Create opportunities for children to develop soft skills, independent thinking, and adaptability.

* Choose a university not only based on reputation, but also on its educational philosophy and ability to support well-rounded growth.

The session concluded with thoughtful and in-depth questions from parents, reflecting strong concern for their children’s future in an increasingly uncertain world.

Though the talk was brief, VinUni Community hopes it provided valuable insights and opened up new perspectives for parents as they walk alongside their children on this journey.

## Chat with VinUnians – Partnering with Your Child in Medical Journey

December 12, 2024

The event “Chat cùng VinUnians – Accompanying Your Child on the Medical Journey,” held on the evening of December 12, 2024, was a resounding success, attracting a large number of parents, students, and members of the VinUni community. Despite being scheduled late in the evening, the program welcomed over 90 parents whose children are pursuing medical degrees at VinUni, as well as many prospective parents considering VinUni for their children.

After the discussion, many parents expressed their satisfaction with the professionalism, valuable information, and meticulous preparation of the event, as well as their appreciation for VinUni’s high-quality education. They shared hopes that the university would continue hosting similar engagement sessions in the future. On behalf of VinUni Community and the College of Health Sciences, we sincerely thank all the enthusiastic participants and supporters of the program.

During the session, faculty members from the College of Health Sciences provided valuable insights into career pathways for medical students at VinUni and introduced the support systems available to accompany them throughout their studies and beyond graduation.

## International-Standard Medical Training Program

The medical training program at VinUni is internationally benchmarked and accredited by the University of Pennsylvania (UPenn). The curriculum emphasizes three core competencies: clinical expertise, professionalism, and research capabilities. Notably, it places significant importance on cultivating empathy, compassion, and humanity in medical practice.

One highlight of the program is its competency-based learning model, which allows students to personalize their academic journey. Students gain early hands-on experience at renowned hospitals such as the 108 Military Central Hospital, Vietnam National Children’s Hospital, and Vinmec Times City. Starting from their fourth year, students participate in full-time clinical internships for 32 hours per week—exceeding the typical hours of other programs in Vietnam.

## Diverse Internship and International Exchange Network

VinUni takes pride in its extensive and high-quality internship network, enabling medical students to gain early exposure to real-world clinical environments. Students have opportunities to train at leading hospitals in both public and private sectors, including the 108 Military Central Hospital, Vietnam National Children’s Hospital, Bach Mai Hospital, the National Hospital of Dermatology and Venereology, and the Vinmec healthcare system (Vinmec Times City is the only JCI-accredited academic hospital in Vietnam). This hands-on experience equips students with essential skills in patient safety, comprehensive quality management, and service-oriented thinking while encountering a wide variety of medical cases to sharpen their expertise.

Moreover, the small-group clinical learning model—limited to just 2-3 students per instructor—ensures close mentorship and supervision, often supported by senior resident doctors. The student management and evaluation system adheres to international standards, employing diverse assessment methods and tools to uphold competency-based learning.

## Postgraduate and Residency Programs Aligned with International Standards

### Doctor + Research Master’s Degree (5+1+1)

VinUni, in partnership with the University of Pennsylvania, offers a unique 5+1+1 model, enabling students to complete a VinUni medical degree and a research master’s degree at UPenn in just seven years. This exceptional opportunity shortens the traditional study timeline while enhancing students’ professional expertise. Eligible students can apply for one of three master’s programs at UPenn (Clinical Epidemiology, Applied Research, or Biomedical Informatics) after completing five years at VinUni. After finishing their master’s degree at UPenn, students return to VinUni to complete their medical degree. This program allows research-oriented students to experience advanced education, learning directly from world-class professors and researchers at UPenn, one of the top medical schools in the world.

### Residency Programs

The residency programs at VinUni are meticulously designed to meet the highest international medical education standards, offering five specialties: Internal Medicine, Pediatrics, Surgery, Orthopedics, and Radiology. Three residency programs (Internal Medicine, Pediatrics, and Surgery) have achieved accreditation from the ACGME-I (Accreditation Council for Graduate Medical Education International), a testament to their quality.

Developed through strategic partnerships among Vinmec, VinUni, and the University of Pennsylvania, these programs aim to elevate medical education standards in Vietnam. As the only residency programs in Vietnam aligned with international standards, they offer future doctors unparalleled opportunities to thrive in a world-class academic environment.

## Pathways to Practicing Medicine in the U.S.

VinUni supports students in building solid careers by preparing them for the United States Medical Licensing Examination (USMLE), an essential step for international doctors seeking to practice in the U.S. Throughout the preparation process, students receive comprehensive guidance from VinUni faculty, equipping them with the confidence and skills to excel in the USMLE. Upon passing the examination, students can apply for U.S. residency programs, unlocking diverse career opportunities in one of the world’s most advanced healthcare systems.

The event concluded successfully, leaving a strong impression on parents and students, reaffirming VinUni’s commitment to supporting the next generation of doctors. We hope parents will continue to join VinUni in future activities, helping to build a holistic learning and development environment for young talents.

Four “deep” lessons emerged from this session, offering powerful inspiration for your academic and scientific journeys:

“What you are doing right now is the most important thing in the world. Don’t worry about the next step—just focus on the present and what’s right in front of you. Whether it’s studying, preparing for an exam, or completing an assignment, this moment is what matters most. Focus and attention are the keys to success, and the way to achieve this is to make the present moment the most important one. Don’t overthink the future!”

“The best research comes from pursuing what truly excites you. Curiosity is the driving force behind great discoveries. Look for fields where most people seem to follow the same path, but you sense something is missing. While you may occasionally be wrong, patience can lead to uncovering the problem and finding the right solution. This is how many groundbreaking studies have come to fruition.”

“Scientific research is a journey of exploration, and we can’t always know where the answers will lie. This is why we need diverse groups, labs, and individuals pursuing different directions. Respecting differences and fostering diversity in research ideas is essential.”

“Set a big goal, then work backward step by step, starting from the very first action. Sometimes, what seems like failure can bring profound insights, and vice versa.”

We believe that VinUni students have truly absorbed the spirit of lifelong learning and resilience exemplified by these scientific pioneers.
