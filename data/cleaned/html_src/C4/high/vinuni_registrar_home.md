### **ABOUT US**

The University Registrar is a key, central and professional service department. The scope of services covers both undergraduate and postgraduate studies. Our mission is to maintain the integrity of the official record of student academic achievement for all colleges by providing academic planning services, data integrity services, and services to current and former students, faculty, staff, administrative staff, and external agencies.

We conserve students’ permanent academic record and provides services for students and faculty members at VinUniversity in the areas of academic policy, enrollment and course information. We act as VinUni’s proxy by coordinating activities which include, but are not limited to, course registration; assignment of classes to classrooms; maintenance of courses offered and grades awarded; certification of students for graduation; and maintenance, protection and release of academic records.

**VinUniversity –  As A Member of AACRAO**

As one of the nation’s oldest and largest higher education associations, AACRAO *(American Association of Collegiate Registrars and Admissions Officers)* is an expert and comprehensive resource for more than 11,000 higher education professionals in the U.S., Canada, and more than 40 other countries.

## 2. Graduation Review Committees

The Graduation Review Process is overseen by Graduation Review Boards at both college and university levels, composed of senior academic leaders, program directors, and administrative representatives.

The committees are chaired by the **Provost**, with participation from the **Vice Provost for Academic Administration**, **Deans and Vice Deans of all Colleges**, **Program Directors of the Colleges** and representatives from the **Student Affairs Management** and the **Office of the Registrar**.

## 3. Graduation Requirements and Eligibility

To be eligible for graduation and the conferral of a bachelor’s degree from VinUniversity, students must satisfy **all applicable academic, administrative, and regulatory requirements** as prescribed by the University and relevant authorities.

### 3.1 Graduation Eligibility Criteria

Students will be considered eligible for graduation upon meeting **all** of the following conditions:

* Completion of the **minimum credit requirements** within the prescribed study period of the program.
* Fulfillment of **General Education/VinCore** and **English language proficiency** requirements.
* Completion of all **major-specific course requirements** as defined by the respective College and Program.
* Resolution of any **“I – Incomplete” grades** in required courses.
* Achievement of a **minimum cumulative GPA of 2.00/4.00**.
* No record of criminal offense up to the time of graduation.
* Compliance with all other applicable **University regulations and policies**.

Students who do not meet all graduation requirements in their intended graduation cycle must apply for graduation again in the following academic year.

### 3.2 Graduation Review Process

The graduation process follows a structured annual cycle:

* **Graduation Application**: Students submit graduation review applications around **April** each academic year, following instructions from the Office of the Registrar.
* **Final Semester Planning**: Students are advised to prioritize remaining required courses and allocate sufficient time for Capstone Projects or Theses, where applicable.
* **Graduation Review**: Graduation eligibility is reviewed at College and University levels, with final decisions typically concluded in **August**.
* **Degree Conferral**: Diplomas and official transcripts are issued after the completion of verification and printing procedures, usually in **September**.

### 3.3 Post–Graduation Review Documents

After graduation eligibility is confirmed:

* Students may request a **Graduation Confirmation Letter** for purposes such as employment, further study, or visa applications.
* Following the issuance of the official **Graduation Recognition Decision** by the Provost, students become eligible to receive a **Temporary (Provisional) Graduation Certificate**.
* The **official diploma and final transcript** will be available approximately one month after the decision.

Students are strongly encouraged to plan ahead if graduation-related documents are required for time-sensitive purposes.

### 3.4 Classification of Awards

Bachelor’s degrees are classified based on the final cumulative GPA, in accordance with **Ministry of Education and Training (MOET)** regulations:

| Classification | Cumulative GPA |
| --- | --- |
| Excellent | 3.60 – 4.00 |
| Very Good | 3.20 – 3.59 |
| Good | 2.50 – 3.19 |
| Marginal | 2.00 – 2.49 |
